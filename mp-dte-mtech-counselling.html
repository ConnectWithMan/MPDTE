Overview of the MP DTE M.Tech Counselling Process

The MP DTE M.Tech Counselling Process is a completely online procedure that facilitates admissions into M.Tech (Master of Computer Applications) courses in Madhya Pradesh. Candidates must participate in this centralized counselling based on their GATE score or Graduation merit.

The process consists of multiple rounds, each involving specific steps that must be completed within stipulated deadlines. Below is a detailed breakdown of each phase of the counselling process.

MP DTE M.Tech Counselling Eligibility Criteria 2026

Before registering for the counselling process, candidates must ensure they meet the minimum eligibility requirements set by DTE MP:

  • Academic Qualification: Must have passed a Bachelor's Degree of minimum 3 years duration with Mathematics at 10+2 level or at Graduation level.
  • Minimum Marks: At least 50% aggregate marks in the qualifying examination (45% for candidates belonging to reserved categories of Madhya Pradesh).
  • Entrance Exam: A valid GATE (NIT M.Tech Common Entrance Test) scorecard is required for the first round of counselling. The second round is typically based on the Graduation qualifying examination merit.

Documents Required for Verification

Candidates must keep the following documents ready in original as well as scanned copies for the registration and institute reporting phases:

  • GATE Scorecard: Required if applying based on GATE score.
  • Class 10th Marksheet: Used as official proof of Date of Birth.
  • Class 12th Marksheet & Graduation Marksheets: All semester/year marksheets are required. Graduation is the primary document for qualifying exam based merit.
  • MP Domicile Certificate: Mandatory for candidates claiming the MP State quota and reservation benefits.
  • Category/Caste Certificate: For SC/ST/OBC candidates (must be issued by a competent authority of MP).
  • Income Certificate: Required for candidates applying under the Tuition Fee Waiver (TFW) scheme or claiming OBC non-creamy layer benefits.
  • Transfer Certificate (TC) & Migration Certificate: Required at the time of final admission reporting at the college.
  • Identity Proof: Aadhar Card or any other valid Govt. ID, along with 4-6 recent passport-size photographs.

Step 1: Online Registration

Registration is the mandatory first step to participate in the counselling.

  • Visit the official DTE MP counselling portal.
  • Register using your GATE Roll Number (or Graduation Roll Number for the qualifying exam round) and Date of Birth.
  • Fill in your personal, academic, and contact details accurately.
  • Upload necessary documents like photograph and signature in the prescribed format.
  • Pay the non-refundable counselling fee online (approx. Rs. 1530/- for unreserved categories).

Step 2: Document Verification

After registration, candidates' documents are verified to confirm their eligibility.

  • In most cases, document verification is done online based on the details and documents uploaded during registration.
  • Candidates will receive an SMS/Email confirming successful verification.
  • If there are discrepancies, candidates might be asked to re-upload documents or visit a help center (nodal center) for offline verification.

Step 3: Choice Filling and Locking

This is a critical step where candidates select their preferred colleges and branches.

  • Log in to your account after successful document verification.
  • View the list of available colleges and branches based on your eligibility.
  • Select and arrange your choices in the order of priority. (It is highly recommended to fill in maximum choices to increase allotment chances).
  • Review the selected choices carefully and lock them before the deadline. If not manually locked, choices will be auto-locked at the deadline.

Step 4: Seat Allotment

Based on merit and choices filled, the authority will release the seat allotment results.

  • The allotment is purely based on the candidate's GATE score or Graduation merit rank, reservation category, and the priority of choices locked.
  • Candidates can check their allotment status by logging into the portal.
  • If a seat is allotted, an Allotment Letter will be generated which must be downloaded.

Step 5: Reporting to the Allotted Institute

Once a seat is allotted and the candidate is satisfied, they must secure their admission.

  • Download the provisional allotment letter.
  • Report to the allotted college within the specified dates along with original documents and photocopies.
  • Pay the requisite admission/tuition fee at the institute to confirm the seat.
  • If a candidate wishes to upgrade their seat in the next round, they can opt for Upward Movement during reporting.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Which score is considered for admission to M.Tech through MP DTE?

MP DTE primarily considers the GATE score for admission. However, if seats remain vacant, admissions are also granted based on Graduation qualifying exam marks.

2. Is there an entrance exam for M.Tech admission through MP DTE?

DTE MP does not conduct its own entrance exam. Admissions are based on the National level GATE examination.

3. Can students from non-Computer Science backgrounds apply for M.Tech?

Yes, graduates from any discipline with Mathematics at 10+2 level or at Graduation level with a minimum of 50% marks (45% for reserved categories) can apply for the M.Tech program.

4. What happens if I forget to lock my choices?

If you forget to lock your choices manually, the system will automatically lock your last saved choices after the deadline expires. This is a critical step, so it's best to lock them yourself to avoid any errors.
